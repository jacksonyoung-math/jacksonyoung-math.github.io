About me

Hello! I'm Jackson Young, a Mathematics Ph.D. student in my third year at Arizona State University. As a kid, I was fascinated by the interplay between mathematics and computer science, something that continues to inform my research to this day. I, 2023, I completed my B.S. in Mathematics at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute in Troy, New York with a minor in computer science. From there, I began studying at ASU in the fall of 2023. I am currently working under the supervision of Julien Paupert, studying arithmetic lattices and complex hyperbolic geometry. You can find a copy of my CV here. In my free time, I like to play music (viola, guitar, and hopefully more instruments soon!), hang out with my cat Leo, and read. Welcome to my web page!

Research

My research, broadly, is focused on arithmetic lattices and hyperbolic geometry. An arithmetic lattice can be thought of as the integer points of a matrix group with finite covolume. Classic examples include ℝn/ℤn, which quotients to the n-torus, and the n × n determinant-1 matrices SL(n; ℤ) in SL(n; ℝ). On the other hand, hyperbolic geometry is the geometry of negatively curved spaces. The geometric structures of hyperbolic and complex hyperbolic geometry provide a rich array of arithmetic lattices to study and learn from. Right now, I am thinking about how to get group presentations for certain arithmetic lattices in the context of complex hyperbolic geometry; that is, lattices in the group of isometries of complex hyperbolic 2-space Hℂ2.
